Vail

Getting There and Away

Vail is located about 34 miles from the Eagle airport and 120 miles west of the Denver airport. Shuttles are available from both airports for $37 and $67 each way, respectively. Fire on over to the Colorado Mountain Express website with this link to book your discounted ride. Otherwise, rent a Caddy and show up in style.

Where to Stay

We highly recommend staying at the Iconic Lodge. A discount is available with the code VG4FRY, just call 1-877-528-7625 to make a reservation. Rooms and Condos start at $230/night.

The Lodge is walking distance to the chapel, welcome party, and the Red Lion. The reception shuttle will depart and return from The Lodge. We do not have a block, so rooms are first come first serve. We recommend booking ASAP as it is Labor Day weekend and all hotels sell out in Vail.

If the Lodge fills up, the discount codes for the The Arrabelle. and The Ritz Carlton. is AG4FRY, just call 1-888-688-8055 to make a reservation. The Arrabell starts at $340/night and the Ritz condos starts at $670/night.

Things to Do

There are so many things to do in the Vail Valley. We hope you come early to enjoy everything Colorado has to offer! Here are links to our favorites:

Labor Day Activities

Gourmet on the Gore Gourmet on the Gore in Vail Village is any foodies paradise. The gourmet food festival runs throughout the entire weekend ending with brunch on Monday.
Vail Jazz Party The Vail Jazz Party is hosted at Vail Square and tickets start at $55/session.
Beaver Creek Oktoberfest We highly recommend grabbing a pint at the Beaver Creek Oktobefest. Dirndls encouraged. Buses are available from Vail to Beaver Creek. If driving, we recommend parking in the Bear or Elk lot at the based of Beaver Creek in Avon and taking the shuttles up. This is a VERY popular event so be prepared for some crowds. (Note, Beaver Creek is only a 15 minute drive away from Vail)
Vail Duck Race Don't be surprised to see thousands of ducks in the river for the annual Vail Rotary club duck race!

Hiking

There is plenty of hiking around the valley. A popular hike is Booth Falls Trail. You can also make the trek to Piney River Ranch (a bit of a drive, recommend a 4wd car) for a spectacular hike around the lake.

Biking

Dan wishes he could join you, but the cycling and mountain biking are pretty epic around the valley.

Outdoor Activities

ATVs, fishing, kayaking, white-water rafting, the list goes on.
